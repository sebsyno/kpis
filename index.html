Marketing Division

Attraction & Acquisition

Cost & Efficiency

Cost Per Acquisition

CPA

How much you spend on marketing to win one paying customer. Lower is better. Because it depends entirely on deal size, it's best judged as a slice of the contract's value.

FormulaTotal Marketing Spend ÷ Total Acquired Customers
The Benchmark
Measured as % of contract valueBadGoodIncredible
Product & Service>50%<33%<20%
Rule of thumb: keep CPA below a third of a customer's first-year value. Read it next to CAC and LTV.

Customer Acquisition Cost

CAC

The full cost of winning a customer — ads plus salaries, software and overhead — not just media spend. The "true" version of CPA.

Formula(Total Sales + Marketing Expenses) ÷ Total New Customers Acquired
The Benchmark
New CAC Ratio ($ spent per $1 new ARR)BadGoodIncredible
Product & Service$2.82 : $1$2.00 : $1<$1.00 : $1
2025 median: $2.00 of sales & marketing to acquire $1 of new ARR (up 14% YoY). Expanding existing customers costs about half as much.

Cost Per Lead

CPL

How much you pay to get one new lead — a contact who showed interest — before they ever buy anything.

FormulaTotal Campaign Spend ÷ Total Leads Generated
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product>$300$150–250<$100
Service>$400$200–350<$150
B2B average ≈ $200; a demo request can cost $600–800. LinkedIn is the priciest channel, SEO and email the cheapest.

Website / Landing Page Conversion

CVR

Out of everyone who visits a page, the share who take the action you want — book a demo or fill in a form.

Formula(Conversions ÷ Total Visitors) × 100
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product (SaaS)<1.8%3–5%>7–10%
Service (Pro Svcs)<2%4–6%>7%
Average B2B site converts ~1.8%. Professional services run higher (4–6%); complex SaaS often lower.

Return on Ad Spend

ROAS

For every $1 you put into ads, how many dollars of revenue come back.

FormulaTotal Revenue from Ads ÷ Total Ad Spend
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
B2B blended<2:14:18:1+
In long B2B sales cycles, ROAS lags — revenue often lands months after the click, so judge it over a quarter.

CAC Payback Period

CAC PB

How many months of customer payments it takes to earn back what you spent to win them. Shorter is healthier for cash flow.

FormulaCAC ÷ (Avg MRR per Customer × Gross Margin %)
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product (SaaS)>18 mo8–12 mo<8 mo
Service (IT)>24 mo12 mo<6 mo
SaaS median ≈ 8.6 months. Sensible targets: <12mo SMB, <18mo Mid-Market, <24mo Enterprise.

Email Engagement

EML

How many people click your emails (CTR) and how many then act (CVR). Ignore open rates — privacy tools have made them unreliable.

FormulaCTR = (Clicks ÷ Emails Delivered) × 100
CVR = (Conversions ÷ Emails Delivered) × 100
The Benchmark
MetricBadGoodIncredible
Marketing CTR<1%2–3.2%>4%
Tech email CVR<1%2.5%>4%
B2B marketing email CTR averages ~3.2%; cold outreach ~5.1%. Email still returns ~$36 per $1 spent.

Click-Through Rate

CTR

Of the people who saw your ad or search listing, the share who actually clicked it. Shows how relevant the creative is.

Formula(Total Clicks ÷ Total Impressions) × 100
The Benchmark
ChannelBadGoodIncredible
LinkedIn Ads<0.4%0.5–1%10–20%*
*Thought-Leader Ads deliver 10–20× the engagement of standard 0.5–1% ad formats.
Marketing → Sales Bridge

Lead → MQL

L2M

Of all the raw leads you collect, the share good enough to be called "marketing-qualified" — a genuine potential fit.

Formula(Total MQLs ÷ Total Raw Leads) × 100
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product (SaaS)<25%31–39%45%+
Service (IT)<17%25–31%40%+
B2B average ≈ 31%; SaaS ≈ 39%; leads from SEO convert best at ≈ 41%.

MQL → SQL ★

M2S

Of your marketing-qualified leads, the share sales agrees are worth pursuing. This is the funnel's weakest point — fix it first.

Formula(Total SQLs ÷ Total MQLs) × 100
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product (SaaS)<13%20–25%35–40%+
Service (IT)<10%15–20%25–30%+
All-industry average is just 13% — 87% of MQLs never become SQLs. Behavioral lead scoring lifts this to 39–40%.

Speed-to-Lead

STL

How fast sales makes first contact after a lead comes in. Minutes matter — speed dramatically lifts conversion.

FormulaAvg ( First Contact Time − Lead Creation Time )
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product & Service>24 hrs<1 hr<5 min
Following up within 1 hour = 53% conversion vs 17% after 24h. Within 5 minutes = 21× more likely to qualify.

Marketing-Sourced Pipeline

MSP

The share of your sales pipeline that marketing created, versus deals sales found on their own. Shows marketing's real revenue impact.

Formula(Marketing-Sourced Pipeline $ ÷ Total Pipeline $) × 100
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product & Service<30%40–50%>60%
A board-level metric. Below 30% means marketing isn't pulling its weight on pipeline.

Sales Division

Conversion, Pipeline & Retention

Conversion & Pipeline

Win Rate

CR

Of the real deals your team works, the share you actually win. Segment matters — enterprise wins less but earns more per deal.

Formula(Closed-Won Deals ÷ Total Created Opportunities) × 100
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product — SMB<20%25–32%35–40%+
Product — Enterprise<12%15–20%25%+
Service (Pro Svcs)<18%22–28%30%+
Average B2B win rate ≈ 21%. A 15% enterprise rate can out-earn a 35% SMB rate thanks to bigger deals.

Sales Cycle Length

SCL

The average number of days from first sales contact to a signed deal. Bigger deals naturally take longer.

FormulaΣ (Close Date − Creation Date) ÷ Total Closed Deals
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
SMB (<$5K)>90 d30–60 d<30 d
Mid-market>120 d60–90 d<60 d
Enterprise (>$100K)>9 mo6 mo<4 mo
SaaS average ≈ 84 days. Deals under $25K ≈ 90 days; deals over $100K run 6–9+ months.

Average Contract Value

ACV

The average yearly value of a new deal — and, just as important, whether that value is growing over time.

FormulaACV = Total New-Deal Revenue ÷ Closed-Won Deals
Growth = ((Current − Prior) ÷ Prior) × 100
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
ACV GrowthDeclining+20% YoY+20%+ w/ margin held
Watch for one "whale" deal hiding a decline across the rest of the book. Always segment by rep and product.

Pipeline Coverage

PCR

How many times bigger your open pipeline is than your sales target — your safety cushion for hitting quota.

FormulaTotal Open Pipeline $ ÷ Quota Target $
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product & Service<2.5x3–4xcoverage × win = quota
3–4x is the rule of thumb, but the real test is simple: coverage × your win rate should equal quota.

Pipeline Velocity

PV

How fast money moves through your pipeline — a single number combining deal count, deal size, win rate and speed.

Formula(Opportunities × Avg Deal Value × Win Rate) ÷ Sales Cycle Length
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product & ServiceDeclining MoM+10% QoQSustained +10% QoQ
SaaS/Tech ≈ $1,847/day ($12.4K deals, 22% win, 67-day cycle). A drop over 10% month-on-month signals deal slippage.

Quota Attainment

QA

The share of your sales reps who actually hit their target. A reliable early warning for morale and turnover.

Formula(Closed Revenue ÷ Quota) × 100
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Market avg.<30% of reps50–60% of reps70%+ of reps
Up to 70% of reps missed quota in 2024; market average ≈ 43%. If fewer than 30% hit, your targets may be unrealistic.

Customer Lifetime Value

LTV

The total revenue you expect from one customer across their whole relationship with you.

FormulaAvg Purchase Value × Avg # Purchases × Avg Lifespan
SaaS: ARR × Gross Margin ÷ Churn Rate
The Benchmark
SegmentSMBMid-MarketEnterprise
Product (SaaS)$15K–40K$80K–200K$300K–1M+
Don't over-trust projected LTV from thin history — apply a haircut until you have 12+ months of real data.

LTV : CAC Ratio

LTV:CAC

How much a customer is worth compared to what you paid to get them. Higher means healthier economics.

FormulaCustomer Lifetime Value ÷ Customer Acquisition Cost
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Product (SaaS)<3:13–4:15:1+
Service (IT)<3:13:14–5:1+
SaaS median ≈ 3.6:1. Below 3:1 you're overspending to grow; 4:1+ means you're ready to scale.
Service & Retention — IT Delivery

Billable Utilization

UTIL

The share of your engineers' paid hours that are billable to clients. The biggest profit driver in a services business.

Formula(Billable Hours ÷ Total Available Hours) × 100
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Consulting staff<65%75–85%85%+
Keep it above 75% weekly. Once non-billable admin time passes 15%, profit erodes fast.

Gross Margin (Services)

GM%

After paying the direct cost of delivering the work, the share of revenue you keep.

Formula((Revenue − COGS) ÷ Revenue) × 100
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Managed Services<40%40–50%50%+
Professional Svcs<50%60%85%+
The average MSP's net profit is ~8–10%; best-in-class reach 15–20%+.

Recurring Revenue Mix

MRR%

The share of revenue that repeats every month (contracts) versus one-off project work. More repeat revenue = a more stable business.

Formula(Recurring Revenue ÷ Total Revenue) × 100
The Benchmark
SegmentBadGoodIncredible
Service (MSP)<50%~60%75%+
The average MSP earns ~60% from recurring services; top performers exceed 75%.
